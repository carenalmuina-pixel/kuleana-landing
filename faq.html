Frequently Asked Questions

Every question, answered honestly.

Public adjusting comes with a lot of fine print and even more uncertainty. Here's the straight, plain-English answer to what we hear most — no sales pitch, just what to actually expect.

01

The Basics

What does my policy actually cover?

More than your carrier may let on. We read your declarations page, exclusions, and endorsements together — and translate the legalese into plain English so you know what you're owed.

My claim was denied. Is it really over?

Almost never. A denial is a position, not a verdict. With proper documentation — photos, moisture readings, a written narrative — most denied claims can be re-opened and challenged, through negotiation or appraisal, or by referring you to coverage counsel if it comes to litigation.

Will hiring you slow my settlement down?

Usually the opposite. A complete, well-documented file moves faster through a carrier's queue than one they can quietly under-pay. We do the work that gets your claim taken seriously.

What does this cost me up front?

Nothing. The initial policy review is free. If there's no recovery, there's no fee.

02

How This Works

What's the difference between you and the adjuster the insurance company sends?

The carrier's adjuster works for the carrier — their job is to manage claim costs on the company's behalf. We're hired by you, and we represent only your interests. Two different adjusters, two different clients.

If the carrier disagrees with your estimate, what happens?

Most disagreements resolve once we present a fully documented, code-compliant estimate. If the carrier still won't move, we look at your policy's appraisal clause or bring in coverage counsel for next steps. You won't be left with only a take-it-or-leave-it offer.

How long does this usually take?

It depends on claim size and how responsive the carrier is — a straightforward reopened claim can resolve in weeks; a heavily disputed one can take months. We'll give you a realistic timeline once we've reviewed your policy and loss, and keep you updated at every step.

Does hiring a public adjuster violate my policy?

No. Hawaii law recognizes a policyholder's right to hire a licensed public adjuster, and carriers are not permitted to penalize you for exercising that right. We're licensed and regulated by the same Hawaii DCCA that oversees your carrier's adjusters.

03

Cost & Payment

How do you actually get paid?

We work on contingency — a percentage of what we actually recover for you, agreed with you in writing before you sign anything. You'll see the exact number up front. There's no flat fee, and no cost to you if we don't recover anything.

Do you get involved with mortgage company or escrow checks?

Yes. Insurance checks on larger claims are often issued jointly to you and your mortgage company, and getting those funds released for repairs can be its own headache. We handle that coordination as part of your file.

04

Your Claim

My claim was already "settled." Can you still help?

Sometimes. If you accepted a payment that didn't reflect the real scope of damage, or new damage has surfaced, we can review whether it's still within your policy's timeframe to reopen or supplement the claim. Send us the paperwork and we'll tell you honestly whether there's a path forward.

What should I gather before we talk?

Your policy declarations page, any correspondence from the carrier, photos or video of the damage, and receipts for emergency repairs like tarping or water extraction. Don't have all of it — we'll help you track down what's missing.
